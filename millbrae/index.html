Comprehensive Termite Inspection

The first crucial step in effective termite control is a detailed and accurate inspection. Our experienced technicians conduct thorough inspections of your property, both inside and out, to identify any signs of termite activity. We meticulously examine all vulnerable areas, including foundations, crawl spaces, attics, wooden structures, and areas with moisture accumulation. Our inspections are designed to detect even the earliest stages of infestation, allowing us to develop the most effective treatment plan.

Advanced Inspection Techniques

We utilize a variety of advanced inspection techniques, including:

  • Visual inspections: Identifying visible signs such as mud tubes, damaged wood, and termite droppings (frass).
  • Moisture meters: Detecting areas of high moisture content, which are attractive to termites.
  • Thermal imaging cameras: Identifying potential hidden termite activity by detecting heat variations caused by termite colonies.
  • Sounding tools: Tapping on wood to identify hollow areas that may indicate termite damage.

A comprehensive inspection by San Mateo Termite provides you with a clear understanding of the extent of the problem and allows us to recommend the most appropriate treatment strategy.

Targeted Termite Treatment

Based on the findings of our inspection, we develop a customized treatment plan tailored to your specific needs. Our goal is to eliminate the termite colony completely while minimizing the impact on your property and the environment. We offer a range of effective treatment options.

Liquid Termiticides

Liquid termiticides are a highly effective method for creating a protective barrier around your property. We apply these products to the soil around the foundation, creating a zone that termites cannot penetrate. This treatment not only eliminates termites that come into contact with the treated soil but also prevents them from entering your home.

Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems are an environmentally friendly and highly effective option. These systems utilize bait stations strategically placed around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting poison that termites consume and carry back to their colony, effectively eliminating the entire colony over time. Baiting systems are an excellent long-term solution for monitoring and controlling termite activity.

Fumigation

In cases of severe or widespread drywood termite infestations, fumigation may be necessary. This process involves enclosing the structure and introducing a gas that penetrates all areas, eliminating termites within the treated space. Our team is highly experienced in conducting safe and effective fumigations, ensuring the complete eradication of the infestation.

Recognizing Termite Problems

Being aware of the signs of a termite infestation is crucial for early detection and preventing significant damage. Termites are often hidden from view, making it difficult to spot their presence until the damage is already underway. However, there are several indicators that suggest you may have a termite problem requiring Termite Control in Millbrae, CA.

Mud Tubes

Subterranean termites build mud tubes, which are pathways made of soil, wood, and their own saliva, to travel between their underground colony and their food source (wood). These tubes are typically found on foundation walls, in crawl spaces, or along wooden structures. Finding mud tubes is a strong indicator of active subterranean termite activity.

Mud tubes provide a protected environment for termites, shielding them from predators and preventing them from drying out. If you discover these earthy, pencil-sized tunnels, it's essential to contact a professional for a thorough inspection immediately. Delaying action can allow the colony to continue feeding and causing damage to your property.

Damaged Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, often leaving the outer surface intact. This can make it challenging to identify termite damage without careful inspection. However, there are often subtle signs that indicate wood has been compromised by termites.

Look for wood that sounds hollow when tapped, or wood that appears discolored or has a honeycomb-like pattern when broken. Termite damage can weaken the structural integrity of your home's framing, flooring, or walls. If you suspect termite damage, it's crucial to have it inspected by a professional to assess the extent of the problem and determine the necessary repairs.

Frass (Termite Droppings)

Drywood termites do not build mud tubes. Instead, they push their fecal pellets, known as frass, out of small holes in the wood. Frass is typically tiny, six-sided pellets that resemble sawdust or coffee grounds. You may find piles of frass near infested wood, often below small boreholes.

Discovering these small piles of pellet-like material is a clear sign of drywood termite activity. The location of the frass can help pinpoint the source of the infestation. Dealing with drywood termites often requires different treatment methods than subterranean termites, so accurate identification is important.

Discarded Wings

Swarming termites, known as alates, are the reproductive members of a termite colony. They emerge from the colony in large numbers to find a mate and establish a new colony. After swarming, they shed their wings. Finding piles of discarded wings, particularly near windowsills or light sources, is a strong indicator of a nearby termite colony and a potential infestation.

Swarming typically occurs during specific times of the year, often after rainfall and when temperatures are warm. While finding discarded wings doesn't necessarily mean your home is currently infested, it suggests that termites are active in the vicinity and increases the risk of future infestation. This is a good time to schedule a preventative inspection.

Clicking Sounds Within Walls

While not as common a sign as mud tubes or frass, some people report hearing faint clicking or tapping sounds coming from within their walls. This sound is sometimes attributed to termites, particularly the soldier termites, who bang their heads against the wood to signal danger to the rest of the colony when disturbed. This sign is more difficult to verify and often requires professional equipment to confirm.

If you are hearing unusual sounds within your walls, especially in conjunction with other potential termite signs, it's worth having a professional inspect the area. While it could be other pests, it's important to rule out termites to prevent potential damage.

Stuck Windows or Doors

This sign is less direct but can sometimes be an indicator of termites in areas with wooden frames. Termite activity and the moisture they introduce can cause wooden window and door frames to warp or swell, making them difficult to open and close. While this can also be caused by normal humidity changes, if it's accompanied by other termite signs, it should be investigated.

If you notice that certain windows or doors in your home are becoming increasingly difficult to operate, and there are no visible signs of swelling due to humidity, consider the possibility of a termite infestation. A professional inspection can help determine if termite damage is the underlying cause.

Visible Termites

Seeing live termites is a definitive sign of an infestation. Subterranean termites are typically creamy white and have a soft body. Drywood termites are also creamy white but may have darker heads depending on their caste. Swarming termites have wings and are often darker in color. If you see termites crawling on your property, especially in or near wooden structures, contact a pest control professional immediately.

It's important to distinguish between termites and ants, as they can sometimes be mistaken for one another. Termites have straight antennae, thick waists, and wings of equal length. Ants have elbowed antennae, narrow waists, and wings of unequal length. If you are unsure, it's always best to have a professional identify the insect to ensure you receive the correct treatment.
